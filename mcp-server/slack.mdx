> **In one line:** Connect Slack to send messages, read channels, and get notified — all from UnleashX.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Authentication** | OAuth |
| **Setup time** | ~2 minutes |
| **Difficulty** | Easy |
| **Best for** | Teams who want agents to post updates or surface messages in Slack |

## 1. Overview

**What is Slack?**
Slack is a messaging app for teams, organized into channels and direct messages.

**What can you do once it's connected?**
Send messages to channels, read recent messages, and list the channels you have access to.

**Why connect it to UnleashX?**
Your agent can post call summaries, notify a channel when something needs attention, or look up a recent message — without you switching apps.

## 2. What you'll need

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- A Slack account in the workspace you want to connect.
- Permission to install or authorize apps in that workspace.

<Warning>
  **Don't have permission to authorize apps?** Some workspaces require admin approval. Ask a Workspace Owner or Admin to approve the connection, or to grant you permission to add apps.
</Warning>

## 3. Get your credentials

Slack uses **OAuth**, so there's nothing to copy. You'll approve access in the next section by signing in to Slack directly.

<Info>
  If your workspace restricts app installs, an admin may need to approve UnleashX once. After that, anyone with permission can connect.
</Info>

## 4. Connect on UnleashX

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Integrations">
    In UnleashX, go to **Integrations** and search for **Slack**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Authorize">
    Click **Connect**, then **Authorize**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in to Slack">
    Choose the workspace, review the requested permissions, and click **Allow**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to an agent">
    You'll return to UnleashX automatically. Assign Slack to the agent(s) that should use it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

✅ **You're connected** when the status shows a green **Connected** badge. The tools below are now available.

### Permissions / scopes requested

| Permission | Why UnleashX needs it |
|------------|----------------------|
| `channels:read` | To list the channels you can access |
| `channels:history` | To read recent messages in a channel |
| `chat:write` | To send messages on your behalf |

## 5. Available tools

| Tool | What it does | Changes data? |
|------|--------------|:---:|
| `list_channels` | List the channels you can access | No |
| `search_messages` | Find messages in a channel | No |
| `post_message` | Send a new message | ✏️ Yes |

> ✏️ = creates or edits data

## 6. Example usage

**Example 1 — Post a summary**

> You type: _"Post yesterday's new signups to the #growth channel."_

What happens: UnleashX uses `list_channels` to find the channel, then `post_message` to send it. You'll see a confirmation when it's done.

**Example 2 — Find a message**

> You type: _"Find the last message from Priya about the launch."_

What happens: UnleashX uses `search_messages` to search and returns the result.

## 7. Permissions & data access

When you connect Slack, UnleashX can:

- Read messages in channels you have access to.
- Send messages as you.

It **cannot**:

- Access private channels you're not a member of.
- Change your account or workspace settings.

You can disconnect at any time from **Integrations → Slack → Disconnect**. Disconnecting immediately revokes UnleashX's access.

## 8. Troubleshooting

| Problem | What it means | How to fix it |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| `not_authed` / `401` | The connection expired or was revoked | Reconnect from **Integrations → Slack** |
| `not_in_channel` | The app isn't a member of that channel | Invite the app to the channel, or post to a channel it's in |
| `missing_scope` | A required permission wasn't granted | Reconnect and approve all requested scopes |
| Can't see a private channel | Private channels require membership | Add yourself (and the app) to the channel |

Still stuck? See [Authentication basics](/mcp-server/authentication) or contact [cs@unleashx.ai](mailto:cs@unleashx.ai).

## 9. Frequently asked questions

**Is my Slack data stored by UnleashX?**
UnleashX reads messages only when a tool call needs them during a conversation; it does not archive your Slack history.

**Can multiple team members connect the same workspace?**
Yes. Each person authorizes separately, so messages are always sent and read with that person's permissions.

**How do I revoke access?**
Disconnect in UnleashX, or remove the app from **Slack → Settings → Manage apps**.
